MentorSHPE

What is MentorSHPE

The MentorSHPE program was created in order to help develop SHPE aggies academically and professionally by pairing its participants to a mentor and/or mentee. Each underclassman will have an upperclassman student assigned to them as a mentor. Each upperclassman will have a mentee appointed to them, and will have the choice to have a professor or a professional from the industry as a mentor. Those interested in going to graduate school should pick the professor as the mentor while those that want to work immediately after graduating should pick the professional as the mentor.

What is the importance of MentorSHPE?

MentorSHPE is very important to SHPE because it helps guide its participants towards academic and professional success. With the help of a mentor, the mentees will be able to gain some direction towards what professors to take, advice for classes, and any other advice/recommendations on educational and professional choices.

MentorSHPE Goals

  1. Provide participants with the guidance to succeed academically and professionally in order to develop them into model students and leaders
  2. Motivate participants to seek long-term mentor/mentee relationships
  3. Inspire participants to see the value of becoming a professional

Definition of Terms

Bigs – Upperclassmen, usually the mentors in the MentorSHPE program
Littles – Underclassmen, usually the mentees in the MentorSHPE program

Students

Requirements of MentorSHPE

  1. Must personally meet with mentor/mentee at least once a month
  2. Attend at least 3 MentorSHPE events/socials per semester
  3. Attend at least 2 workshop with mentor/mentee per semester

Professors

Program Overview & Schedule

Professor mentors will be appointed to participants of the same, or similar, field of study. The form of communication will be left up to the mentor and mentee; however, consistent communication is encouraged by meeting at least bi-weekly. Topics and a list of questions will be provided to guide the discussions.

Professor Mentor Benefits

  1. An experience where your efforts impact an individual’s life
  2. Ability to develop communication, interpersonal, leadership, and coaching skills
  3. Opportunity to give back to the community; opportunity to share experience and knowledge gained through similar challenges and experiences

Requirements of Professor Mentors

  1. Communicate with mentee at least twice a month
  2. Meet with mentee in person at least once
  3. Guide the mentee as needed and provide constructive feedback
